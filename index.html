About the Series

The Lord of the Rings is a fantasy novel series written by J.R.R. Tolkien. It tells the story of the hobbit Frodo Baggins and his journey to destroy the One Ring, a powerful and evil artifact created by the dark lord Sauron.

The series is set in Middle-earth, a fictional world created by Tolkien. It features a wide range of characters, including hobbits, humans, elves, dwarves, and wizards, as well as various creatures such as orcs, trolls, and dragons.

The series has been widely popular and influential, and has been adapted into numerous other media, including films, video games, and stage productions.

Books

The Lord of the Rings consists of three volumes: The Fellowship of the Ring, The Two Towers, and The Return of the King. The books were originally published in 1954, 1955, and 1956, respectively.
